    STC8A Development Records

    MiniScope based on STC8A

    Last year I noticed a project named MiniScope on Bilibili video platform. It really attracted me on account of its low cost and relatively simple engineering difficulties.

    I want to record my development process because I haven’t built and complete an embedded project on my own. This development record will help me understand the STC8Ax chip and other fundamental modules on embedded system.

    Introduction

    I think this post should serve as an outline. Therefore, no details will be shown in this post but development timeline or brief summary of some problem I met.

    But I think, at initial stage, simple records about the tips or somewhere needs to pay attention to is necessary. I will reorganize this post when it looks a bit mess.

    2022-06-01

    Data Truncation

    In STC8A chip, data can not be declared to double or float
    There exist two scenario in which the data truncation occurs.

    In ADC conversion, accurate reference voltage is important. STC8A calibrates the internal reference level and stores the result in RAM. In STC8A8K64S4A12, we can locates this value in RAM address 0xEF(High Byte) and 0xF0(Low Byte).

    The following formula indicates a way to use the REFV in RAM calibrating the VCC in embedded system.

    ADCRAMBandGapADCBandGapSampled/4096=ADCCalibratedVoltageADCSampledVoltage/4096×Resistance_Divider_Coefficient\frac{ADC_{RAMBandGap}}{ADC_{BandGapSampled}/4096} = \frac{ADC_{CalibratedVoltage}}{ADC_{SampledVoltage}/4096} \times Resistance\_Divider\_Coefficient ADCBandGapSampled​/4096ADCRAMBandGap​​=ADCSampledVoltage​/4096ADCCalibratedVoltage​​×Resistance_Divider_Coefficient

    Take care the (uint32_t) forced type conversion and the division process locating at the tail.

    • In STC8A all variables are integers so I initially expand ratio to one hundred times.
    • unit16_t type multiplication process may result in data overflow, so (uint32_t) conversion is necessary.
    • Do not use () to imitate the math formula above. For instance, (ratio/100) if ratio=590, the result will be 5 other than you imaged effect as 5.9. So I give multiplication process higher priority and abandon the () in calculation.
